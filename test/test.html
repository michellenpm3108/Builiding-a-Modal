COVID-19 [portmanteau words]

coronnial

/kəˈroʊ.ni.əl/

Someone who was born around the time of the covid-19 pandemic.

covidivorce

/ˌkoʊ.vɪ.dɪˈvɔːrs/

The process of ending a marriage as a consequence of the couple spending a large period of time together in lockdown during the covid-19 pandemic.

double bubble

/ˌdʌb.əl.ˈbʌb.əl/

The people from two separate households who are allowed to see each other as part of the gradual easing of restrictions during the covid-19 pandemic.

social bubble

/ˌsoʊ.ʃᵊl.ˈbʌb.ᵊl/

A small group of family and friends who are permitted to see each other as the COVID-19 lockdown measures are gradually eased.

elbow bump

/ˈel.boʊ ˌbʌmp/

A friendly greeting in which you touch someone's elbow (= the place where the arm bends) with your elbow.

lockdown tache

/ˌlɑːk.daʊn.ˈtæʃ/

A moustache that its wearer has allowed to grow during lockdown.

maskne

/ˈmæsk.ni/

Acne caused or made worse by wearing a mask.

Zoomwear

/ˈzuːm.wer/

A style of dressing that involves wearing clothes suitable for the office above the waist and casual clothing below the waist.

quaranteen

/ˈkwɔːr.ən.tiːn/

A teenager in the time of the covid-19 lockdown.

quaranteam

/ˈkwɔːr.ᵊn.tiːm/

A group of people who go into quarantine together.

twindemic

/twɪn.ˈdem.ɪk/

A widespread outbreak of both flu and covid-19 at the same time.

long covid

/ˈlɑːŋ.koʊvɪd/

A condition in which people who have had the covid-19 virus continue to have symptoms and feel unwell for a long time.

covexit

/ˌkoʊ.ˈveg.sɪt/

The process of easing the restrictions on public life imposed by the COVID-19 pandemic.

air bridge

/ˌer.ˈbrɪdʒ/

A flight route between two countries where the covid-19 virus is well controlled, enabling people to travel without having to go into quarantine afterwards.

anti-masker

/ˌæn.t̬i.ˈmæskɚ/

A person who opposes wearing a face mask during the COVID-19 pandemic.

space marshal

/ˈspeɪs.mɑːr.ʃᵊl/

someone whose job is to make sure people are obeying the rules of physical distancing in places such as shops, libraries etc.

revenge spending

/rɪˈvendʒ.ˈspen.dɪŋ/

The activity of shopping more than usual as a reaction to not having been able or allowed to do so for a period of time.

spendemic

/spenˈdem.ɪk/

A sudden tendency for people to spend money, usually on unnecessary things.

lockstalgia

/lɑːk.ˈstæl.dʒə/

A feeling of nostalgia for the lockdown period of the covid-19 pandemic.
